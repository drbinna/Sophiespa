# Sophie Spa — Ultimate Figma Make Prompt

---

## BRAND IDENTITY

**Name:** Sophie Spa
**Tagline:** Premium mobile beauty and wellness experience — delivered to your door, anywhere across Auckland.
**Tone:** Serene, luxurious, organic, warm — but unmistakably modern. Think Japanese minimalism meets Aesop meets a high-end Auckland day spa.
**Color Palette:** Soft blush pink (#F5E6DC), warm cream (#FDF8F4), muted rose/mauve (#C4929B), deep charcoal (#2C2C2C), antique gold accents (#B8956A). White space is a design element — use it generously.
**Typography:** Use an elegant serif for headings (think Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or similar high-contrast serif) paired with a clean geometric sans-serif for body text (Inter, Satoshi, or similar). Headings should feel editorial and magazine-quality. Body text should feel airy and effortless.

## PAGE STRUCTURE & SECTIONS

Design a single-page scrolling website with the following sections, top to bottom:

---

### 1. NAVIGATION BAR (Sticky)
- Minimal sticky top nav with frosted-glass/blur background effect on scroll
- Left: "SOPHIE SPA" wordmark in serif, letterspaced
- Center or right: nav links — About, Treatments, Pricing, Team, Testimonials, Contact
- Far right: "Book Now" pill button in muted rose with hover glow effect
- Nav should feel weightless — thin, transparent, barely there until you scroll

---

### 2. HERO SECTION ⭐ (THIS IS THE STAR — MAKE IT SLEEK AND CINEMATIC)

**THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT SECTION. It must feel like a luxury fashion editorial crossed with a wellness film opening.**

**Layout:** Full-viewport height (100vh). Split or layered composition.

**The Spinning Bowl Animation (CRITICAL):**
- Feature a large, photorealistic stone/marble spa bowl (like the one in the brand's existing imagery — a wide, shallow stone vessel on a wooden pedestal) as the centerpiece
- The bowl should have a **continuous slow 360° rotation animation** — smooth, hypnotic, almost meditative. Think product showcase rotation like Apple or high-end ceramics brands
- The bowl floats in the center or slightly off-center on a clean, minimal background
- Subtle particle effects around the bowl: tiny floating rose petals, soft golden light flecks, or gentle steam wisps that drift upward
- The rotation should feel weightless and calming — not fast, not mechanical. Ease-in-out, perpetual, like watching incense smoke curl

**Supporting Hero Elements:**
- Large editorial headline overlaid or beside the bowl: "Where Stillness Meets Your Skin" or similar evocative copy — massive serif type, possibly with a subtle fade-in-up animation on load
- Subheadline: "19 years of expertise. Premium mobile wellness. Delivered to your door across Auckland."
- A single CTA button: "Book Your Escape" — pill-shaped, muted rose, with a soft shimmer/glow hover animation
- Background: very subtle gradient from warm cream to soft blush, or a barely-there texture (linen, paper grain)
- Optional: a thin horizontal scroll indicator or downward arrow with a gentle bounce animation at the bottom of the viewport

**Motion & Feel:** Everything in the hero should animate in gracefully on page load — staggered fade-ins, the bowl already spinning when it appears. The overall impression should be: *calm authority*. This is not a budget spa. This is an experience.

- Use thin horizontal dividers between items, not heavy borders

## GLOBAL DESIGN DIRECTIVES

- **White space is sacred.** Every section should breathe. Generous padding (80–120px vertical) between sections.
- **Animations:** Use scroll-triggered animations throughout — fade-in-up for text, scale-in for images, staggered reveals for card grids. Keep all animations subtle and slow (300–600ms with ease-out). Nothing should feel jumpy or flashy.
- **Micro-interactions:** Buttons glow or shift color on hover. Links have smooth underline animations. Cards elevate on hover. The spinning bowl is the hero moment.
- **Responsive:** Design mobile-first. On mobile, the spinning bowl should still be prominent in the hero, scaled down but centered. Cards stack vertically. Nav collapses to hamburger.
- **Photography style:** Warm, golden-hour lighting. Natural textures (wood, stone, linen, botanical). Skin tones should look healthy and radiant. No harsh studio lighting.
- **Accessibility:** Ensure sufficient contrast on all text. Interactive elements should have clear focus states.
- **Overall vibe:** If Aesop, Aman Resorts, and a Kyoto ryokan had a digital baby raised in Auckland — that's Sophie Spa.

## KEY INTERACTIVE MOMENTS TO PROTOTYPE

1. **Hero bowl spin** — continuous 360° rotation, smooth, hypnotic
2. **Sticky nav blur transition** — transparent → frosted glass on scroll
3. **Treatment card hover** — subtle lift + image zoom
4. **FAQ accordion** — smooth expand/collapse
5. **Scroll-triggered section reveals** — staggered fade-in animations
6. **CTA button hover** — shimmer or glow pulse

---

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Generic spa website templates — no teal, no stock waterfall images, no cheesy wellness clichés
- Heavy gradients, neon accents, or overly saturated colors
- Cluttered layouts or tiny text
- Flat/static feel — every section should have at least one subtle motion element
- The word "PLACEHOLDER" appearing anywhere in the final design — replace with realistic content or elegant lorem ipsum
